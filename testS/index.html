Higher education in Ukraine

Start the process!

Higher education in Ukraine has long and rich history. Ukrainian students, graduates and scientists have long been known and appreciated around the world. The first disclosure and innovative researches of scholars, who worked in Ukrainian institutes and academies, such as Dmytro Mendeleev, Mykola Zhukovsky and Yevgen Paton, are part of the general history of scientific development of the world.

In May 2005 Ukraine joined the Bologna Process. The Bologna process positively effects on the integration of Ukrainian education into a single European educational space.

Currently, there are about 240 higher schools in Ukraine that prepare specialists for foreign countries, and offer a wide range of specialties in various fields of knowledge for foreign students. The doors of Ukrainian universities are opened annually for the students from more than 150 countries of the world. Studying is held in Ukrainian, Russian and English.

Foreigners are enrolled into educational institutions under accredited educational programs twice a year, before and at the beginning of academic semesters (until November 1 and until March 1, respectively): for obtaining degrees of Junior Bachelor, Bachelor.

To postgraduate, doctorate, clinical residence, medical residency, programs of preparatory faculty (department) and programs of academic mobility – during the year.

Ukraine

is a state in Eastern Europe and partially in the Central Europe, at the south-western part of the East-European Plain. This is the country with the largest area of those which territory is completely situated in Europe[5], the second on the European continent, if to consider Russia. It borders with Belarus in the north, Poland, Slovakia and Hungary in the west, Romania and Moldova in the south-west, Russia in the east and north-east. In the south and south-east is washed by Black and the Azov Seas.

By the number of population of Ukraine holds the eighth place in Europe. In Ukraine there live more than 48.4 million people. The official language in Ukraine is Ukrainian. Most of the population belongs to the Christians-Orthodox and Greek Catholics. The middle confessions are presented by Roman Catholicism, Protestantism, Islam.

Ukraine is a unitary state. It consists of 24 regions and the Autonomous Republic of Crimea. The capital of the country is Kyiv. The official currency is hryvnia (UAH).
